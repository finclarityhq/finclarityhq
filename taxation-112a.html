01. The New 12.5% Regime

The Union Budget 2024 redefined the rules of the wealth game. By increasing Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) from 10 percent to 12.5 percent, the fiscal drag on a 20-year portfolio has effectively increased by 25 percent. This is not a linear loss; it is a Compounding Disruption. Every rupee paid in tax today is a rupee that cannot compound for the next two decades.

However, the budget also introduced a tactical pivot: the increase of the annual exemption limit to ₹1,25,000. For the institutional architect, this is not a suggestion—it is a mandate to perform Exemption Harvesting. Our data shows that for a ₹50 Lakh portfolio, consistent harvesting can add over ₹18 Lakh to the terminal value by simply resetting the cost basis every financial year.

02. The Harvesting Algorithm

Exemption Harvesting is the mechanical process of selling profitable units to "capture" the tax-free gain of ₹1.25 Lakh and immediately reinvesting that capital back into the same or similar asset. This process resets your Cost of Acquisition (CoA) to the current Market Price, effectively "erasing" that profit from future tax calculations.

Cost Basis Reset Equation

\[ CoA_{new} = \text{NAV}_{current} \] \[ \text{Tax Shield} = 1,25,000 \times 0.125 = ₹15,625 / \text{year} \]

While ₹15,625 in annual tax savings seems marginal, the Wealth Physics of this maneuver is profound. By not paying that tax today, you allow the full corpus to compound. Over 30 years at 12 percent growth, these small annual shields aggregate into a massive capital advantage. The execution must be precise: you must sell units that have completed 365 days (to qualify for LTCG) and ensure the total booked profit does not exceed the limit to avoid the 12.5 percent leakage.

03. Section 112A & Grandfathering Math

For investors holding equity assets acquired before 2018, Section 112A provides a critical legal protection known as the Grandfathering Clause. This ensures that gains made prior to the reintroduction of LTCG in 2018 remain tax-free. The calculation is technically complex and requires a three-step price floor logic.

The Grandfathering Logic

Your Cost of Acquisition for pre-2018 units is the higher of:
1. Actual Purchase Price
2. Lower of: (a) Fair Market Value on Jan 31, 2018 OR (b) Sale Price.

Institutional Note: This ensures you never pay tax on a "notional" gain if the market price was higher in 2018 than your eventual sale price.

Professional tax planning requires a per-unit audit of your portfolio. Many investors mistakenly pay tax on grandfathered gains because their broker's P&L Statement does not correctly calculate the Jan 2018 price floor. A manual audit of CoA under Section 112A is mandatory for any portfolio exceeding ₹1 Crore.

04. Tactical Loss Harvesting

While Exemption Harvesting captures gains, Loss Harvesting captures the "Value of a Red Day." During market drawdowns—such as the 2020 pandemic or the 2022 rate hikes—portfolio architects sell underperforming assets at a loss. This loss is then used to offset the taxable gains of winners.

In the Indian tax code, Short-Term Capital Losses (STCL) are the most powerful tool because they can be adjusted against both STCG and LTCG. Long-Term Capital Losses (LTCL), however, can only be adjusted against LTCG. Crucially, any unadjusted loss can be carried forward for 8 years. This creates a "Tax Shield" that you can deploy during future bull markets to exit large positions tax-free.

The Offset Hierarchy

STCL can offset:

● 20% STCG
● 12.5% LTCG

LTCL can offset:

● 12.5% LTCG ONLY

05. Asset Location Strategy

Traditional finance focuses on Asset Allocation (Equity vs. Debt). Institutional finance focuses on Asset Location (Which account holds which asset). To minimize tax drag, you must place your "high-friction" assets in tax-exempt or tax-deferred wrappers.

For example, Fixed Deposits (FDs) are now taxed at your income slab (up to 39%). Holding a debt fund is similarly inefficient in 2026. Instead, the architect uses Arbitrage Funds for the debt portion of the portfolio. Arbitrage funds deliver debt-like returns but enjoy Equity Taxation (12.5% LTCG). This single shift in "Location" can increase your post-tax yield by 2.5% to 3% per year—a massive delta when compounded over a lifetime.
