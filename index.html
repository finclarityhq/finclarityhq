1. The Physics of Systematic Wealth Creation

In the volatile landscape of the Indian equity markets, a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) acts as a mathematical Volatility Harvester. By deploying a fixed amount of capital at regular intervals, an investor decouples their wealth from the anxiety of market timing. This utilizes the Rupee Cost Averaging principle: you buy more units when prices are depressed and fewer when they are elevated.

Mathematically, the average cost per unit in a SIP is the Harmonic Mean of the purchase prices. In any market cycle with variance, the Harmonic Mean is strictly lower than the Arithmetic Mean, giving the disciplined investor a systematic cost-advantage over time.
